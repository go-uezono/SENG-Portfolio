Requirements

A month has passed since starting my software class, and so far, it has come to be one of my favorites. Being able to code again brought light to my motivation (since this past year and a half have been full of computational theory), albeit being rusty with Java. Content delivered in the class so far has been new – such as UML diagrams, use cases, etc. – but taking theory courses prior helped me in understanding the content a lot easier and faster, thus am really looking forward to obtaining new knowledge in future deliverables.

This first assignment allowed me to utterly understand how I, as an individual, work in a team. To lay out my strengths – I believe I was adequate in keeping up with deadlines, addressing what/when tasks needed to be complete, and catching smaller detailed portions that may have been missed by the team. After completing our first meeting, we set soft deadlines for everyone to work on the assignment on their own first. That way, we were able to figure out similarities between our diagrams and things that one of us might have missed, as well as staying on top of things – having some leeway with time constraints. This was where I was able to take the stage – implementation of “choose not to bag” was the one that was included in mine but not the others. We were all able to combine our diagrams and come to a group consensus on what should be added/removed. Communicating our ideas and reasoning for this was handled well by everyone in the group, which made this assignment a smooth sail.

In contrast to my strengths, there were several noticeable drawbacks I learnt about myself, the major one being well-reserved when we’re sharing our thoughts or giving input on some phenomena. I recall myself doing my own thing often – whether that be coding, working on a question of an assignment, etc. – which makes it harder for me to get my point across. Some part of me fears being incorrect, which leads to the fear of being judged. Quite often there will be times I wait till someone else gives their first thought, which then ends up being like the idea I had in mind. In that case, I leave it at that and agree with whoever said it. Another trait I need to inherit myself is leadership. Taking on the initiative to organize a group/team is something looked for in the working industry, especially in an environment where individuals will be working in groups. Throughout this assignment, I realized I end up conforming with the group a lot, which isn’t bad but shouldn’t always be the case.

Overall, the direction our group took on this assignment was the most optimal – it allowed us to explore our own ways of solving problems first, then reconvening and sharing our work with one another. This way, we have input on how we thought it worked, where others may think differently. It’s always good to have a second opinion when you’re unsure of something. This approach also really made me realize how groups operate, and how I operate in a group setting. The listed strengths will be something I will keep up for future assignments, and the weaknesses I’ve encountered about myself will be something I’ll be more aware of when I have colleagues I’m working with.
